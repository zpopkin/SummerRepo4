Being an educator affords the opportunity to be a lifelong learner, and whether it is through a professional development opportunity, or a book that I am reading, I am always looking for ways to bring new skills or knowledge into my classroom.  This approach helps to engage learners, as well as myself, in our collaboration.  The idea of developing lessons around hypothesis and regression testing is incredibly appealing.  I am always looking for ways to evaluate information and test hypotheses in my own life.  This course would provide me with a new skill set that I can then develop in my students.  

My teaching philosophy is to apply core concepts taught in the classroom to real world scenarios. One example of how I currently couch these ideas in class is through an analysis of genetic sequences.  Learners were asked to pull a series of DNA sequences from the NIHs BLAST database, they then created a phylogenetic tree that illustrated the extent to which certain sequences were related to one another in order to determine whether or not a crime occurred.  Additionally, in the summer of 2020, I was fortunate enough to attend a camp through the National Institutes of Health National Human Genome Research Institute (NHGRI), where  several of the panelists highlighted how important programming and computer modeling are to all of their work.  As a result of these discussions I knew I had to add to the lab outlined above.  Students began the year by running a similar BLAST analysis where they investigated the mutations of the SARAS-CoV-2 virus.

If selected for this program, my ultimate goal would be to use this knowledge and experience to create authentic experiences in the classroom, as well as work with school leadership to refine the data analysis that we use to inform pedagogical and administrative decisions.  Data analysis and hypothesis testing are incredibly important aspects to all science.  Taking this course through American University would allow me to further develop the data analysis done in  my classes.  Additionally, I would use this content in my capacity as Science Department Chair.  I would help colleagues develop their own hypothesis & regression testing into their classes as well as apply this work to  analysis of student data across multiple curricula.
DCPS Science Lead
